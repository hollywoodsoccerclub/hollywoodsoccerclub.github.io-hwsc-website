Kinderkick U6:

  1. FUN: Passion for the game.

  2. Technical mastery.
    • Main focus is ball touches and dribbling in a fun environment.
    • One ball. One child. Complete focus on the individual.
    • Develop big muscle groups with balance as a focus.
    • Skipping and running.
    • Ball Touches:
      • http://www.youtube.com/user/hollywoodsoccerclub

  3. Practice Plans: 60 minutes:
    • Welcome 5 minutes.
    • Ball touches 10 minutes.
    • Dribbling 10 minutes.
    • Play small side games. 1v1 1v 2, and 2v2. 15 minutes.
    • Fun games 15 minutes.
    • (Dribbling Games) Keep away, tag, treasure hunt in ones. Maze games. (Fun Games) Asteroid, 3 v 3.

U7/U8

  1. FUN: Passion for the game.

  2. Technical mastery.
    • Main focus is receiving and passing, with ball touches and dribbling included in a fun environment.
    • One ball. One child as much as possible in the first half of practice.
    • Ball Touches: Toe touches on top of ball. Pull backs. Side to side, keeping low. http://www.youtube.com/user/hollywoodsoccerclub

  3. Introduction of teamwork with 2s:
    • Solving problems in groups of two.

  4. Offensive and Defensive concepts introduced:
    • Everyone plays offensive and defense within a position.
    • Emphasize concepts over positioning.

  5. Practice Plans: 60 minutes:
    • Coordination Training 5 minutes.
    • Ball touches 5 minutes.
    • Dribbling 5 minutes.
    • Passing/Receiving 10 minutes.
    • 2 v 2 or 2v2, plus 1, for 10 minutes.
    • Fun games 10 minutes.
    • Closing 5 minutes.

U9/U10

  1. FUN: Passion for the game.

  2. Technical mastery.
    • Main focus is passing and receiving techniques, with ball touches and dribbling included, shooting introduced.
    • Ball Touches: V, behind the back V. Pull, Push Stay low http://www.youtube.com/user/hollywoodsoccerclub
    • Shooting Video:
      http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u8-JyBrcO9A&feature=related

  3. Introduction of small group activities:
    • Working in 3s and 4s to solve problems.
    • 3v1.
    • Keep Away with every player touching ball once.
    • 1, 2, 3, 4 passing.

  4. Offensive and Defensive concepts introduced as group play in 3s and 4s.
    • Develop players through multiple positions.
    • Offensive: Attack, expand / possession, creativity
    • Defensive: Pressure, Cover, Balance. Chorography

  5. Practice Plans: 60 minutes:
    • Jog/light stretching 5 minutes.
    • Ball touches 5 minutes.
    • Dribbling 10 minutes.
    • Passing/Receiving 10 minutes.
    • Shooting 5 minutes.
    • 2 v 2 or 2 v3, or 3 v 3 15 minute games.
    • Fun games or 4 v 4 or 6 v 6 for 15 minutes.

  6. Special Notes:
    • Motivation:
    • Ask Children to "Show me"
    • Do drills twice and time them to see if the child can improve.
    • Boys /Girls:
    • Boys want to be top dog.
    • Girls want to be nice and make friends.
    • Teach girls to compete.
    • Teach boys to help make their teammates better.

U11/U12

Goals

Technical mastery:

  • Main focus is passing/receiving and shooting techniques, with ball touches and dribbling included. Ball touches against Defense. Heading introduced.
  • Small group activities continue: Working in larger groups to solve problems.
  • Offensive / Defensive team concepts introduced. Develop players through multiple positions.
  • Ball Touches: http://www.youtube.com/user/hollywoodsoccerclub

Practice Plans: 60 minutes

  • Jog/light stretching 5 minutes.
  • Ball touches 5 minutes.
  • Passing and or receiving 10 minutes.
  • Shooting 10 minutes.
  • 2 v 2 or 2 v3, or 3 v 3 15 minute games.
  • Fun games or 4 v 4 or 6 v 6 for 15 minutes.

U13/U14

Goals for Practice

  1. Fun for the players
  2. Lots of touches on the ball
  3. Practice keeping the ball on the ground
  4. Building relationships with the players, coaches and families

Practice Organization

  • Divide the practice time into two parts: @75% for time at @three stations, and @25% for a scrimmage.
  • Divide players so that there are two teams at each station at any given time.
  • Rotate among stations so that each group gets equal time at each station.
  • If a planned exercise at a given station isn’t working for your group, then improvise!
  • Remind your players that this practice is about control, not power, and keeping the ball on the ground is a good thing.

Station 1: 4 Goal Game

Teams can score in any goal. In order to win, a team must score in each of the 4 goals. Coach restarts the drill when the ball goes out of play and after goals are scored.


Alternative 4 Goal Game

Split into 4 teams. Use 2 balls. One pair of teams plays against each other, scoring on the North and South goals. The other pair of teams plays against each other, scoring on the East and West goals. This teaches awareness of surroundings, communication, tight touches and playing with one’s head up.


Station 2: Double Hot Box

Mark a "goal box" with cones a few yards off each end line. Teams score by controlling the ball in the goal box. Starting in one box, a team can only score in the other box, but once crossing midfield, they can score in either box. This drill encourages playing with the head up and making quick decisions.


Progressions for Double Hot Box

Only score by completing a pass into the box (dribbling in does not count); at least N players must touch the ball before scoring.


Station 3: Ball Skills & Wambach Heading Technique

Use the drills shown in this video with Abby Wambach demonstrating her heading technique.
